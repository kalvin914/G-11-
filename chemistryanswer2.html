3. Answer all questions (15 marks)

  1. When ethanol is heated with concentrated sulphuric acid at 170°C:
    • The reaction is a dehydration reaction, producing ethene (C₂H₄).
    • Equation: C₂H₅OH → C₂H₄ + H₂O
  2. When sodium propanoate is heated with soda-lime:
    • The reaction is a decarboxylation reaction, producing ethane (C₂H₆).
    • Equation: CH₃CH₂COONa + NaOH → C₂H₆ + Na₂CO₃
  3. When ethanol vapor is passed over freshly heated copper at 300°C:
    • The reaction produces ethene (C₂H₄) by dehydration.
    • Equation: C₂H₅OH → C₂H₄ + H₂O

5. Layers of the atmosphere and the thermosphere

List major layers of the atmosphere and explain the thermosphere:

  • Troposphere
  • Stratosphere
  • Mesosphere
  • Thermosphere

The thermosphere is the layer of the atmosphere located above the mesosphere. It contains a high concentration of ions and is where auroras occur. The thermosphere is characterized by a rapid increase in temperature with altitude due to the absorption of high-energy solar radiation.

7. Oxygen conversion to ozone and calcium carbide reaction:

  1. Oxygen can be converted to ozone by applying UV light or electric discharges:
    • O₂ → O + O (UV radiation splits O₂ molecules into oxygen atoms)
    • O + O₂ → O₃ (Formation of ozone)
  2. Gas X is obtained by treating calcium carbide with water:
    • Gas X is acetylene (C₂H₂), an unsaturated hydrocarbon.
    • Equation: CaC₂ + 2H₂O → C₂H₂ + Ca(OH)₂
    • Acetylene is an unsaturated hydrocarbon because it contains a triple bond (C≡C).
